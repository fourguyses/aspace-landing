The problem

The data needed to feed the world is already up there. It just hasn't been made useful.

Traditional soil testing costs €20–30 per sample, takes weeks to return results, and covers only a fraction of farmland — leaving most agricultural decisions based on guesswork. The consequence is an estimated €2 trillion in annual losses from yield gaps and wasted inputs.

Meanwhile, satellites orbit the Earth daily, capturing spectral data that contains everything needed to map soil chemistry at field resolution. aSpace exists to close that gap.

Our technology

SoPrAna — Soil Properties Analysis.

SoPrAna is an Earth Observation-based digital soil mapping pipeline. It fuses multi-source satellite imagery with ensemble machine learning — trained on over one million ground-truth soil samples — to predict 20+ soil parameters from a single farm coordinate in under 10 seconds.

The system generates physical soil chemistry, nutrient profiles, uncertainty maps, and fertiliser recommendations at field resolution, continuously updated with no physical sampling required. Accuracy consistently reaches 90% against independent lab benchmarks.

Our mission

Accelerate farmer prosperity.

We believe that giving farmers, cooperatives, and land managers access to real soil data — not estimates, not averages, but field-level intelligence — is one of the highest-leverage interventions available in global agriculture.

Precision soil data reduces input waste, increases yields, enables carbon revenue, and helps meet the regulatory requirements shaping farming in the EU and beyond. That's what we're building toward: a world where every farm, everywhere, has access to the soil intelligence it needs to thrive.
